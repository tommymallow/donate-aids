How Does Compassion Help?

Donations to the Compassion AIDS initiative are used to provide support to children infected by AIDS, living with the HIV virus, or who are living with parents or siblings who are HIV+ or infected by AIDS. Compassion uses your donations to provide:

  • Education that prevents the spread of HIV
  • Transportation to clinics for treatments
  • Treatment with antiretroviral therapy (ART)
  • Nutritional support for children who are infected with HIV
  • Education and medication for the Prevention of Mother-To-Child Transmission
  • Voluntary counseling and testing

Everyday, we Help Children Like Godfrey

Godfrey's name and identity have been changed to protect his privacy.

An orphan at the age of 12, Godfry began exhibiting HIV symptoms only a few short days after his mother's death.

Godfry was so devastated by the news that he tried to hang himself. Thankfully, the rope snapped and social workers at his Compassion-assisted child development center immediately intervened. Today, through the support of Compassion's AIDS program, he is on antiretroviral therapy (ART) and is defying the disease.

Because of your donations, children like Godfry are able to fight back against the devastating spread of AIDS.
